# Creative Brief: apleith.com — Copy & Structure Redesign

**Prepared for:** Web team
**Project:** apleith.com personal site redesign
**Objective:** Convert visitors into collaborators — industry partners, consulting clients, grant co-investigators, academic collaborators, and prospective advisees

---

## The Problem with the Current Site

The current site is a well-formatted CV. It documents credentials but does not sell them. Someone who lands on it learns *what Alex has done* but leaves without a clear sense of *what they could do together* or *how to start*. There are no distinct engagement paths, no "who this is for" framing, and no conversion moments beyond a PDF download.

The goal is to reframe the site from a credential archive into an **active invitation to work together**.

---

## Audience Personas

The site serves four distinct visitors. Each needs a different message.

| Persona | Why they're here | What converts them |
|---|---|---|
| **Industry researcher / platform team** (gaming, social media, tech) | Evaluating whether Alex's expertise is the right fit for a study, audit, or advisory engagement | Proof of domain depth + concrete examples of past industry work |
| **Academic collaborator / grant partner** | Exploring co-authorship, co-PI opportunities, conference partnerships | Publication record + active grant pipeline + shared methodology |
| **Consulting client** (media org, startup, policy group) | Looking for someone who can design a study and deliver actionable findings | Track record framed as outcomes, not methodology |
| **Prospective grad student / lab member** | Deciding whether to apply to SIUE's program | Research culture, current projects, approachability |

## Recommended Page Structure

### Section 01 — Hero

**Purpose:** Orient the visitor and name the offer in one sentence. Don't open with a title. Open with the work.

**Proposed headline direction:**
> *Behavioral research for platforms that want to understand their users — not just measure them.*

**Subhead:** Names Alex clearly — name, title, institution. One sentence. Not a paragraph.

**Body (2–3 sentences max):** Establishes the dual identity — tenured researcher + practicing consultant. Names the domain (social platforms, gaming, VR/XR, trust & safety). States that formal partnerships with Riot Games and VRChat are ongoing.

**CTAs (two):**
- Primary: "Work with me" → scrolls to engagement section or mailto
- Secondary: "Download CV" → PDF

**What to cut:** The current about-bio is good but too long for a hero. Move the substance into the body of the page.

---

### Section 02 — What I Work On

**Purpose:** Give visitors a fast way to self-identify. Replace the current Skills tag cloud with substantive domain framing.

**Structure:** Four topic areas as short paragraphs (not bullet lists, not tags). Each is one strong sentence naming the area + one sentence describing what kinds of questions get answered + one sentence of real-world applicability.

**Domains to cover:**

1. **Social VR & XR environments** — how people form relationships, experience presence, and behave in virtual spaces; foundational for platform design, onboarding, and safety policy
2. **Trust, safety & community governance** — harassment typologies, hate speech detection, moderation effectiveness; relevant to trust & safety teams and policy groups
3. **Parasocial media & behavioral analytics** — how audiences form bonds with streamers, creators, and characters; data from Twitch (321M+ messages), Riot Games (5M+ records)
4. **Mixed-methods research design** — surveys, experiments, interviews, NLP corpus analysis, and structural topic modeling, often combined in a single study; relevant to anyone who needs a finding that will hold up

**What to cut:** Skills section as currently structured. Tag clouds convey breadth without depth and read as filler. The domains above do the same work with authority.

**Format:** A tight stats strip followed by 3–4 brief highlights.

**Stats to include:**

- $1.3M+ — active grant pipeline (pending)
- 321M+ — behavioral data records processed
- 13 — peer-reviewed publications from NSF project alone

**What to cut:** The employment timeline read-through. Move it to the CV PDF. This section earns attention faster with density and specificity.

### Section 04 — Work With Me *(new section — critical)*

**Purpose:** This section does not exist in the current site. Its absence is the single biggest reason a visitor leaves without converting. It must answer: *what kinds of engagements are available, and how do I start one?*

**Intro line (1 sentence):** Something like: *Available for a focused set of engagements where research depth and technical execution both matter.*

**Four engagement types (label + 1–2 sentences each):**

1. **Research Consulting**
   Applied behavioral research for product, policy, or strategy teams. Study design through final deliverable — methods, analysis, and findings you can use.

2. **Grant Partnerships**
   Seeking co-PI and co-investigator relationships for NSF, NIH, Wellcome Trust, or foundation proposals. Particularly interested in human-computer interaction, platform behavior, and AI-augmented environments.

3. **Speaking & Teaching**
   Conference keynotes, executive briefings, and workshops on social platforms, trust & safety, VR/XR behavior, and computational research methods.

4. **Software Licensing**
   Research tools built at SIM DAD LLC — including TASS (text analysis) and Ibis (transcription) — available for institutional and commercial licensing. See [simdadllc.com](https://simdadllc.com).

**Contact bar:** One clear line.
`hello@simdadllc.com · aleith@siue.edu`
No form. No friction.

**Purpose:** Validate academic standing without burying the visitor in a full bibliography.

**Format:** Three publications, each with:
- Full citation (styled)
- One-sentence plain-language gloss of what the finding means

**Recommended selections:**
1. PLOS One — strong methodological breadth or social impact
2. IEEE Transactions on Games — signals gaming/platform credibility to industry
3. Communication Monographs — signals top-tier academic standing

**Trailing CTA:** "Full publication list and CV →" links to CV PDF or a publications subpage

---

## Tone & Voice

- Confident, not self-promotional. State facts; let them speak.
- Precise, not jargon-heavy. Write for an intelligent non-specialist as the floor — industry PMs, program officers, department chairs.
- Direct. No rhetorical questions. No "I'm passionate about..." No filler.
- The bio should read as someone who has done the work and is still doing it — not someone who is summarizing a tenure file.

---

## What the Redesigned Site Should Make Possible

By the end of a visit, a reader should be able to answer:

1. What does he actually study?
2. Has he done this for organizations like mine?
3. What would I hire him to do?
4. How do I reach him?

The current site answers #1 and partially #2. It fails #3 and #4.
